Overview

Selamat Datang to Langkawi

Lying off the northwest coast of Kedah where the Andaman Sea meets the Strait of Malacca, Langkawi is an archipelago of 99 islands (104 at low tide) granted UNESCO Global Geopark status for its 550-million-year-old rock formations.

It is Malaysia's most loved island getaway, and a duty-free zone, so chocolate, perfume and a sunset cocktail all come cheap. Ride the Panorama SkyCab to the summit of Gunung Machinchang, walk out over the rainforest on the curved Sky Bridge, then unwind on the soft sands of Pantai Cenang. From mangrove safaris to eagle-feeding cruises, jom, let's explore.

Top Things To Do

From sky-high views to mangrove rivers, here's how to make the most of your days on the island.

Cable Car & Sky Bridge

Soar 708 m up Gunung Machinchang on the Panorama SkyCab, then stroll the curved 125 m Sky Bridge suspended above the rainforest.

Island Hopping

Speedboat to Pulau Dayang Bunting's freshwater Lake of the Pregnant Maiden, watch eagles dive, and beach-hop to Pulau Beras Basah.

Pantai Cenang

The island's liveliest beach, golden sand, parasailing and jet skis by day, beach bars and grilled seafood at sunset.

Kilim Mangrove Tour

Glide through the Kilim Karst Geoforest Park past limestone cliffs, bat caves and brahminy kites in their natural habitat.

Eagle Square

Snap the iconic 12 m Dataran Lang sculpture, a giant reddish-brown eagle that gives Langkawi (the Eagle Isle) its name.

Underwater World

One of Asia's largest aquariums, walk a glass tunnel beneath sharks and rays, then meet rockhopper penguins in the Antarctic zone.

Good to know

Best Time & Quick Facts

Best Season

December to April, dry, sunny days and calm seas, ideal for island hopping. Avoid the wetter September-October monsoon.

Getting There

Direct flights to Langkawi International Airport (LGK) from KL in ~1 hour, or a 1.5-hour ferry from Kuala Kedah or Penang.

Currency

Malaysian Ringgit (RM). Langkawi is duty-free, so alcohol, chocolate and souvenirs are notably cheaper than the mainland.

Language

Bahasa Malaysia is official, but English is widely spoken across hotels, tours and restaurants, getting around is easy.
